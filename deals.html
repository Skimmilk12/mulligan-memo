Kill ListLeft on the cutting-room floor

Every morning the bot surfaces the loudest “discounts” on the internet. The loudest ones are usually lying. The current rejects — excluded from every page of this desk:

  • “94% off” E6 APEX simulation software ($2,500 → $150) — That $2,500 anchor is a commercial-license price no home user ever paid. Against the home license it replaces, the cut is real but far smaller. Fake anchor — skipped.
  • “88% off” E6 Connect software ($2,500 → $300) — Same $2,500 commercial-license anchor as its APEX sibling. The software is real; the discount arithmetic is not. Skipped.
  • “60% off” E6 Connect Home software ($2,500 → $1,000) — Third member of the same anchor family — a home license measured against a commercial-license price. Skipped.
  • “89% off” Voice Caddie SC200 practice bundle ($3,098 → $349) — The “was” price is a bundle misconfiguration — the SC200 is a ~$300 device on its best day, and the product URL literally ends in “-copy.” Skipped.
